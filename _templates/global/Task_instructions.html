üß± Basics

On your screen, you will see 16 blue squares. Each square represents a hidden card which can be revealed by clicking on it. Your task is to find as many matching pairs of cards as possible within the time limit.

üïπÔ∏è How to play

  1. Start with a set of 16 face-down cards.
  2. Click any two cards to flip
    • If they match, you earn one point.
    • If not, they flip back.
  3. Once you found all the 8 pairs, you get a new set of 16 cards. You can play up to 7 sets per round (the maximum score is 56).

üéØ What is a match

The rule for matching pairs depends on the difficulty level of the cards.

  1. Easy level: Each card shows an addition problem (e.g. 2+3). Two cards form a matching pair fit their solutions are the same (e.g. 2+3 = 4+1).
  2. Hard level: Each card shows an addition problem and a Roman numeral (I or II). Two cards form a matching pair if both the sum and Roman numerals are the same (e.g. 2+3 II and 4+1 II).
