On this page, you will see the instructions showed to each worker. You will get to practise this task but your performance in the task does not affect your payoff. Understanding the task is important for making your selection decision and thus maximising your payoff.

üß± Basics

On your screen, you will see 16 blue squares. Each square represents a hidden card which can be revealed by clicking on it. Your task is to find as many matching pairs of cards as possible within the time limit.

üïπÔ∏è How to play

  1. Click on a card to flip it over and see what's on it.
  2. Click on another card to flip it over.
  3. If the two cards match, they will disappear.
  4. If they do not match, they will flip back over and be hidden again.
  5. Repeat the above to find as many matching pairs in the time.

üéØ What is a match

The rule for matching pairs depends on the difficulty level of the cards.

  1. Easy level: Each card shows an addition problem (e.g. 2+3). Two cards form a matching pair if their solutions are the same (e.g. 2+3 and 4+1 both equal 5).
  2. Hard level: Each card shows an addition problem and a Roman numeral (I or II). Two cards form a matching pair if both the sum and Roman numerals are the same (e.g. 2+3 II and 1+4 II).

You will see one set of cards at a time. Each set contains 8 pairs (16 cards). After you find all 8 pairs, a new set appears. You can play up to 7 sets per round (the maximum score is 56).

üèÜ Timing & Scoring:

  • Each round lasts two minutes (120 seconds).
  • You earn 1 point per correct match.
  • The number of points you earn will affect your bonus.

Keep in mind that performance in this task is influenced by your maths skills.
